## 1. Product Summary

Gauntlet is a social fitness app that combines the gamification depth of Duolingo, the social mechanics of Strava, and the ranked progression systems of competitive gaming (League of Legends, Valorant, chess) into a single product built for anyone who moves.

**Core insight:** The best accountability partner isn't a coach or an app notification — it's a friend you're trying to beat. Combine competition with social identity (squads, campus pride, rankings) and deep gamification, and you create a product people don't just use but brag about.

**What makes it different:** No existing product combines deep gamification, tiered ranked competition, squad mechanics, multi-platform wearable integration, and shareable social content in a single app designed for everyone from casual walkers to competitive athletes.

## 2. Target Audiences

Three overlapping audiences, prioritized in this order for launch:

**Primary — The Social Fitness Casual:** College students, friend groups, coworkers who download because their squad invited them and stay because the gamification is addictive. Dense social networks, high social comparison, high group identity. This is the viral distribution engine.

**Secondary — The Wellness Journey User:** People focused on weight loss, step counts, or building a consistent movement habit. They want accountability, not intimidation. The tiered ranking system ensures they compete against peers at their level.

**Tertiary — The Competitive Athlete:** Runners, cyclists, swimmers who already track everything on Strava or Garmin and want a new dimension of competition beyond segment leaderboards.

## 3. Core Feature Set

### Friend vs. Friend Competitions
Head-to-head or multi-person fitness challenges. Users set activity type, metric (distance, calories, steps), and timeframe. Real-time progress updates and push notifications.

### Squads
Groups of 2–10 friends competing as a unit against other squads of the same size and rank. Both group and individual leaderboards within a squad. Social accountability layer — you don't just let yourself down, you let your squad down.

### The Ranked System
(See Section 4 for full details.) Tiered system from Bronze through Obsidian. Every user gets a rank based on activity output, consistency, and competitive results. The emotional backbone of the product.

### Shareable Social Content
Every competition result, rank promotion, streak milestone, and squad victory generates a shareable card optimized for Instagram Stories, TikTok, and X. Primary organic distribution mechanism. Should be as distinctive as Spotify Wrapped.

### Data Visualizations
Weekly mileage curves, step count heatmaps, activity breakdowns by sport, squad performance trends, win/loss records, rank progression charts, head-to-head historical comparisons.

### Gauntlet Tournament (Simplified at Launch)
Direct matchups between two same-size groups in a one-week head-to-head challenge. Real-time progress visibility, in-app chat between competing groups. Purpose-built for fraternities, sororities, club sports teams, dorm floors, corporate teams.

### Global and Opt-In Leaderboards
Segmented by activity type, time period, geography, and rank tier. Opt-in only — no forced exposure.

### Weight and Body Composition Tracking
Optional, always private by default, never surfaced on leaderboards. The app competes on activity output, not body metrics.

## 4. The Ranked System

### Tier Structure

| Rank | Percentile | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Bronze | Bottom 40% | New users, just starting |
| Silver | 40th–60th | Consistent casual athletes |
| Gold | 60th–80th | Dedicated athletes, strong weekly output |
| Platinum | 80th–90th | High-performing, competing seriously |
| Diamond | 90th–97th | Elite performers |
| Titanium | 97th–99th | The best of the best |
| Obsidian | Top 1% | The pinnacle. Born from volcanic fire, impossibly sharp, dark and rare. |

Each tier has subdivisions (e.g., Gold I, Gold II, Gold III).

### Rank Calculation

Composite score weighting three factors:
- **Volume:** Total activity output in a given period
- **Consistency:** How regularly the user trains (daily/weekly streaks)
- **Competitive Results:** Win/loss record in head-to-head and squad competitions

This composite approach prevents gaming through single massive efforts and rewards sustained, consistent behavior.

### Why Tiers Matter

Solves the critical problem that kills most fitness leaderboards: discouragement. Segmenting users into rank tiers means every competition feels winnable, every promotion feels earned. Also creates aspirational identity — players describe themselves as a "Diamond player" the way gamers do.

## 5. Monetization

### Freemium Model (No Ads)

Free tier includes all core competition features, basic data visualizations, squad participation, and the full ranked experience.

### Gauntlet Pro — $4.99/month

No competitive advantage — free users compete on a level playing field. Pro unlocks depth and personalization:
- Unlimited active competitions (free: 2–3)
- Advanced analytics, exportable reports, performance predictions
- Unlimited challenge creation with custom rules (free: 1 per month)
- Custom cosmetics: badges, profile themes, squad banners, rank emblems, shareable card themes

### Cosmetic Microtransactions

Limited-edition seasonal badges, animated profile borders, squad logos, exclusive card themes. Cosmetic only — never competitive advantage.

### Pricing Philosophy

$4.99 is deliberately impulse-friendly for college students. No-ads commitment is deliberate — Gauntlet should feel premium from day one. Network-dependent value means freemium ensures maximum distribution velocity.

## 6. Integrations

Gauntlet does not ask users to manually log workouts. All data flows automatically.

### Core Integrations at Launch

| Platform | Coverage |
|----------|----------|
| Apple Health / HealthKit | iPhone and Apple Watch users |
| Strava | Runners and cyclists |
| Garmin Connect | Serious endurance athletes |
| Google Fit / Health Connect | Android wearable users |
| Fitbit | Casual users and wellness community |

### Manual Entry Policy

Manually entered workouts appear in private activity feed but do NOT count toward leaderboards, ranks, or competitions. Preserves integrity while letting users maintain complete personal records.

### Supported Activities at Launch

Running, cycling, swimming, weightlifting, walking/steps. Plugin-based architecture for adding new sports.

| Fitbit Challenges | Step challenges, wellness community | Fitbit-only, minimal gamification |

## 8. Distribution Strategy

Ordered from highest-leverage/lowest-cost to more scalable:

1. **College Campus Playbook:** Campus leaderboards, inter-campus rivalries (Michigan vs. Ohio State, Duke vs. UNC), Greek life integration (pledge classes, IFC tournaments), campus ambassadors at 10–20 target universities.

2. **Referral Program:** Tiered rewards modeled on Morning Brew's milestone structure. 3 referrals = exclusive cosmetic. 5 = free month of Pro. 10 = limited-edition badge. 25 = physical merch. Fraud prevention: referred users must verify and log at least one real activity.

3. **Social Media Distribution:** Organic content loops through shareable cards (Spotify Wrapped-level ubiquity goal). Platform-native content on TikTok, Reels, Shorts. Creator and micro-influencer outreach (10K–100K followers).

4. **Community Seeding:** Authentic posts on Reddit (r/running, r/cycling, r/fitness, university subs), Discord fitness communities, Facebook Groups.

5. **Personal Networks:** First 500–1,000 users through direct outreach via fraternity, sorority, club sports, and campus organization networks.

## 9. Future Features

These are planned for post-launch. Do not describe these as current features.

**Campus Organization Competitions (Paid B2B):** IFCs, Panhellenic Councils, residence halls pay for structured multi-week bracket tournaments. "Get Ready for Summer" seasonal branding. Optional charity integration for institutional buy-in.

**B2B Corporate Wellness:** Companies pay per-seat for branded internal challenges. Limited to activity-output metrics only (steps, distance, active minutes). Weight, calories, body composition explicitly excluded from any corporate product.

**AI-Verified Exercise Challenges (V2):** On-device computer vision counts reps (pushups first, then squats, pull-ups, etc.). "1,000 pushups in a month" challenge format. Inherently shareable content with live rep counter overlay.

**Gauntlet Full League Structure (V2):** Squads placed in leagues, weekly matchups through structured seasons, promotion/relegation system.

**Other Future Considerations:** Influencer Olympics (tournament brackets with follower communities), streak repair mechanic (deliberately excluded at launch — streaks should have real stakes), annual Gauntlet Wrapped recap, sleep/recovery data integration, wager/stake competitions (requires legal review), physical merch and trophies for seasonal champions.
