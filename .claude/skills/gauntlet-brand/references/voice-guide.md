## 1. Voice Identity

Gauntlet's voice is **confident, high-energy, sharp, and competitive.** Every word earns its spot, or it gets cut. No fluff, no filler, no corporate warmth. The tone sits at the intersection of Nike copywriting, gaming patch notes, and sports media — a coach who talks trash but backs it up.

The voice should feel like it comes from someone who competes, not someone who watches from the sidelines. It's the difference between "We believe in the power of fitness" (corporate, passive, weak) and "Your squad's down 2,000 steps. Get moving." (direct, competitive, alive).

### Voice Coordinates

Think of Gauntlet's voice as living at a specific intersection:

- **Nike's copywriting** — declarative, muscular, aspirational without being soft
- **Gaming patch notes** — precise, informational, respects the audience's intelligence
- **Sports media** — stats-forward, competitive framing, rivalry energy
- **The group chat** — casual confidence, trash talk as affection, shared references

Gauntlet is NOT:
- A wellness brand (no soft, nurturing tone)
- A corporate app (no "we're excited to announce")
- A health app (no clinical or medical register)
- A lifestyle brand (no aspirational vagueness)

## 2. Voice Principles

### Confident, not arrogant.
Gauntlet knows what it is. The copy doesn't hedge, apologize, or explain itself. But it never punches down — the energy is "you belong here and you're about to surprise yourself," not "you're not good enough yet."

**Yes:** "Gold III. You earned it. Now defend it."
**No:** "Congratulations on your amazing achievement! We're so proud of you!"

### Crisp, not clever.
Short sentences. Active voice. If a sentence can lose a word and still land, lose the word. Wordplay is welcome when it's sharp, but cleverness for its own sake gets cut.

**Yes:** "14 days straight. The streak is yours now."
**No:** "You've been on a roll — literally and figuratively! Keep that streak streak-ing!"

### Competitive, not hostile.
Gauntlet celebrates competition as play, not war. Trash talk is fun, not mean. The vibe is the group chat after a pickup game, not an internet argument.

**Yes:** "Your squad's down 2,000 steps. Get moving."
**No:** "Your squad is losing badly. Don't be the weak link."

### Inclusive through action.
Gauntlet doesn't lecture about inclusivity — it demonstrates it. The ranked system ensures every user competes against peers at their level. The copy reflects this: it speaks to effort, consistency, and showing up, not genetic gifts or athletic pedigree.

**Yes:** "Every rank earned. Every step counted."
**No:** "Whether you're a beginner or elite athlete, Gauntlet is for everyone!"

### Data-forward.
Gauntlet speaks in numbers. Stats, records, streaks, ranks — the data IS the copy. When in doubt, lead with the number.

**Yes:** "Last week: 3 wins, 1 loss, 47 miles. This week's yours."
**No:** "You had a great week of activity! Keep pushing forward!"

## 3. Vocabulary Rules

### Words We Use

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| Climb | Implies upward progression, effort, ranked tiers |
| Forge | To create through heat and pressure |
| Earn | Everything in Gauntlet is earned, not given |
| Squad | The team unit — casual, gaming-native, inclusive |
| Crew | Alternative to squad in informal contexts |
| Compete | The core action |
| Battle | Elevated stakes — squad vs. squad, Gauntlet matchups |
| Grind | Sustained effort over time — positive connotation |
| Track | What the app does with your data |
| Prove | Competitive motivation — prove it with numbers |
| Show up | The minimum viable action — respect for consistency |
| Rank up / Level up / Promote | Tier progression language |
| Lock in | Focus, commitment — native to the target demographic |

### Words We Never Use

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| Journey | Overused wellness-speak. Gauntlet is about competition, not journeys. |
| Tribe | Appropriative and dated. Use squad or crew. |
| Challenge yourself | Too soft, too inward-facing. Gauntlet challenges you against others. |
| Wellness / Mindfulness | Wrong register entirely. Gauntlet is not a wellness app. |
| Optimize / Hack / Biohack | Silicon Valley performance-bro language. Not Gauntlet's audience. |
| Empower | Corporate buzzword. Gauntlet doesn't empower — it competes. |
| Community | Overused to the point of meaninglessness. Be specific (squad, campus, leaderboard). |
| Excited | "We're excited to..." is the most generic opening in tech. Never use it. |
| Incredible / Amazing / Awesome | Empty superlatives. The data speaks for itself. |
| User | In external copy, say "you" or "athletes" or "competitors." "User" is internal only. |

### Punctuation and Formatting Rules

1. **No exclamation marks in primary copy.** They undercut confidence. A period after a strong statement is more powerful than an exclamation mark after a weak one.
2. **Periods over ellipses.** End with certainty, not trailing off.
3. **Contractions always.** "Don't" not "do not." "You're" not "you are." Gauntlet is casual-confident.
4. **Em dashes sparingly.** One per piece of copy maximum. Prefer periods for separation.
5. **Numbers as numerals.** Always "3 wins" not "three wins." "47 miles" not "forty-seven miles." Numbers are visual — they should look like data, not prose.

## 4. Copy Patterns by Context

### Push Notifications
The most constrained format. Max 2 lines. The first line delivers the information; the second line delivers the motivation or action.

**Pattern:** [Fact/stat]. [Short imperative or observation.]

Examples:
- "Your squad's down 2,000 steps. Get moving."
- "Gold III. You earned it. Now defend it."
- "14 days straight. The streak is yours now."
- "You've been drafted. Don't let them down."
- "No active challenges. That's a choice."

### Onboarding Screens
Short, sequential, action-oriented. Each screen should have one sentence or phrase max. Progressive revelation — don't explain everything at once.

**Pattern:** [Imperative verb]. [Context or payoff.]

Examples:
- "Pick your squad. Pick your sport. Start climbing."
- "Connect your watch. We'll handle the rest."
- "Every workout counts. Every step tracked."

### Empty States
Empty states are opportunities for competitive motivation, not apologies. Never say "nothing here yet" passively.

**Pattern:** [Observation about the empty state]. [Implication or challenge.]

Examples:
- "No active challenges. That's a choice."
- "No squad yet. You're competing alone."
- "Clean slate. First move is yours."

### Weekly Recaps
Data-dense, stat-forward. Lead with the record or the headline number. Support with secondary stats.

**Pattern:** [Time frame]: [Hero stat], [supporting stats]. [Forward-looking closer.]

Examples:
- "Last week: 3 wins, 1 loss, 47 miles. This week's yours."
- "March recap: Gold II → Gold III. 12 competitions. 187 miles. Keep climbing."

### App Store Description
Longer form but same voice. The first sentence is everything — it needs to communicate what Gauntlet is in one punchy line. Subsequent copy can expand, but every sentence still earns its place.

**Opening line pattern:** [What Gauntlet does] + [emotional payoff].

Example opening: "Gauntlet turns every workout into a competition, every friend group into a squad, and every athlete into a ranked contender."

### Marketing / Social Media Copy
Same voice as in-app. Don't soften it or formalize it for external channels. The brand voice IS the marketing.

**Pattern varies by format:**
- **Tweet/X post:** One punchy line. Stat or provocative statement. No hashtag spam.
- **Instagram caption:** 1-2 sentences max. Let the visual (shareable card) do the work.
- **TikTok text overlay:** Ultra-short. 5-8 words max.

### Investor / Pitch Materials
The voice can shift to a more analytical register here. You can use longer sentences and more complex constructions. But the core identity holds: confident, data-forward, no buzzwords, no hedging.

**What changes:** Sentence length, analytical depth, market data inclusion.
**What doesn't change:** Confidence, directness, specificity, avoidance of corporate buzzwords.

## 5. Anti-Patterns

Copy that should never come out of Gauntlet, with corrections.

### The Corporate Announcement
- "We're excited to announce our latest feature that we think you're going to love!"
- "Squad Gauntlet is live. Pick your roster. Find your matchup."

### The Wellness App
- "Remember, the most important competition is with yourself. Take care of your body and mind."
- "Your only rest day opponent is yesterday's you."

### The Apology
- "We're sorry for any inconvenience. We're working hard to fix this issue."
- "Leaderboards are down. Fix incoming. Your stats are safe."

### The Empty Superlative
- "You had an incredible week! Amazing job hitting your goals!"
- "3 for 3 this week. Undefeated."

### The Hedge
- "If you're looking for a way to maybe get more active, Gauntlet might be right for you."
- "You compete. You climb. That's Gauntlet."

### The Lecture
- "At Gauntlet, we believe that fitness is for everyone regardless of ability level, and we've designed our platform to be inclusive and welcoming."
- "Bronze to Obsidian. Everyone starts somewhere. Everyone climbs."

### Streak Milestones
- "14 days straight. The streak is yours now."
- "30-day streak. A month of not quitting."
- "7 days. Week one locked in."
- "100 days. Triple digits. Undeniable."

### Squad Copy
- "You've been drafted. Don't let them down."
- "Your squad's down 2,000 steps. Get moving."
- "Squad victory. 14,200 to 12,800. That margin was you."
- "5 members. 1 squad. Nobody coasts."

### Competition Results
- "W. 8,400 to 6,200. Not close."
- "L. 11,300 to 12,100. 800 steps. That close."
- "3 wins, 1 loss this week. The record speaks."

### Gauntlet Tournament
- "Matchup set. Your 12 vs. their 12. One week. Total distance wins."
- "Gauntlet result: W. Your crew outran them by 23 miles."
- "New Gauntlet drops Monday. Roster locks at midnight."

### Onboarding
- "Pick your squad. Pick your sport. Start climbing."
- "Connect your watch. We'll handle the rest."
- "Your first rank: Bronze. Everyone starts here. Not everyone stays."

### Marketing Headlines
- "Compete. Climb. Conquer."
- "Every workout has a winner."
- "Your squad is waiting."
- "Ranked fitness. For real."
- "The scoreboard doesn't lie."

### App Store / Website
- "Gauntlet turns every workout into a competition, every friend group into a squad, and every athlete into a ranked contender."
- "The best accountability partner isn't a coach or an app notification. It's a friend you're trying to beat."
- "Track everything. Compete against anyone. Climb from Bronze to Obsidian."
